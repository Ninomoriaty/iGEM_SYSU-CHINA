Description

Although China and many developed countries have spent huge sums of money and a lot of manpower on research to overcome cancer, the "incurable disease" cancer still seriously threatens the health of human beings. According to the National Cancer Center of China, in 2014, the number of new cases of malignant tumors in China reached 3.804 million (2.114 million males and 1.69 million females), and an average of 7 people per minute were diagnosed with cancer [1]. The infinite ability of tumor cells to replicate, sustained angiogenesis, immune tolerance and other characteristics have led to clinical treatment difficulties [2]. Therefore, there is an urgent need for a new type of therapy that has a good effect on preventing tumor recurrence and metastasis. This therapy is expected to be less prone to drug resistance, safe and reliable, with mild side effects, and at the same time improve the tumor's own anti-tumor immune function.


miRNA

MicroRNAs (miRNAs) are a class of non-coding single-stranded RNA molecules of approximately 22 nucleotides in length encoded by endogenous genes. Such RNAs play a role in post-transcriptional regulation of gene expression, and they are powerful regulators of various cellular activities, including regulation of cell growth, differentiation, development, and apoptosis.

Currently, miRNAs have been shown to be abnormally expressed in different cancer types and can play an important role in resembling oncogenes or tumor suppressor genes. In recent years, the rapid development of molecular medicine has led to the discovery and application of a large number of tumor markers in clinical practice, which has led to a significant increase in the early diagnosis rate and prognosis of many tumor diseases.
